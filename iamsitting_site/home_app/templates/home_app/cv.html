Personal Statement

Nothing more than technology has allowed us to redeem our time, so I would like to reciprocate by giving my time back. There is a psalm that goes on to say "The days of our years are seventy years, or if because of strength, eighty years." For some, like my mother, that number is less; consequently, I have learned to treasure a gift which we all are given: time. I have decided to allot the next few years of my life to pursue a doctoral degree in a field that not only befits my tastes, but also contributes to the general welfare of man.

My interests lies in the internet-of-things and smart devices. My first taste of it was in my internship at Siemens where I was working on energy managing smart devices. My project involved the communication protocol between an in-house smart device and a third party smart device. The project was so invigorating that I went from never having touched a line of Python code to writing Python REST APIs in a weekend. Eventually, through the project's use of openADR, I was introduced to smart grids and directed the remaining of my undergraduate studies towards wireless networks and communications.

Many are quick to note my 2-year hiatus in my CV. So this begs the question: how did I go from smart grids to Bible school? At the end of the day, I care for people and I want to serve people. Some people have Habitat for Humanity, Doctors without Borders, etc., but Bible school is how I wanted to devote my time. Now that I am finishing my program, I am looking to consummate all that I have picked up in my time at Texas A&M University and at the FTTA by pursing a Ph. D in eletrical engineering. In my search, I found the Wireless Networking, Signal Processing, and Security Lab at the University of Houston as a promising institution to profit from my time.
